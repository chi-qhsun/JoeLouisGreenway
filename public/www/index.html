Joe Louis Greenway Overview

The Joe Louis Greenway is a 27.5-mile urban trail connecting multiple Detroit neighborhoods, as well as Highland Park, Hamtramck, and Dearborn. Named for legendary boxer and civil rights advocate Joe Louis, the trail is designed to promote safe, accessible routes for walking and biking, while revitalizing communities and fostering local economic growth.

Warren Gateway

The $6 million Warren Gateway segment marks the first constructed trailhead on the Detroit–Dearborn border. It features a four-acre park west of Central Avenue, with a playscape, a pavilion for community events, fitness equipment, green infrastructure, and accessible parking. This new space was funded by a $3 million grant from the Ralph C. Wilson, Jr. Foundation and $3 million in city bond funds.

Facilities at Warren Gateway

Amenities include picnic areas, shelters, water fountains, playgrounds, parking, and bike-sharing stations—fostering a welcoming environment for visitors of all ages.
